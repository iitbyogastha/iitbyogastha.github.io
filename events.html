We are what we eat

The IIT Bombay’s Yogastha club organized a talk on “We are what we eat” for campus residents on September 24, 2021. The talk by Mrs Forum Devang Shah pressed the importance of eating right and adopting a healthy lifestyle for holistic living. The speaker began the talk by discussing the three different doshas and how it determines an individual's body and mind state. The ancient health science of Ayurveda is built around the five elements of ether, air, fire, water, and earth. The unique combination of these elements come together to make up the doshas, or constitutions—vata, pitta, and kapha. Each dosha has a number of corresponding qualities that are expressed in the physical, emotional, and mental aspects of one’s being. All of us have some aspects of each dosha, but one or two doshas tend to dominate, informing everything from our digestion to our emotions. The speaker also threw light on the types of food that should be consumed and avoided during different seasons of the year. Over 70 participants attended the online session.

The Art of Harnessing Mind Power

Shri Radheshyam Das Ji began the session by explaining the fundamentals of body, mind, and soul and how we should never forget that the soul keeps changing the body and acquiring states of mind. He asked the participants to identify their nature based on pure innate qualities and bad acquired qualities based on Three Gunas – Sattva, Rajo, and Tamo Guna with various situations and stories posed to the audience. People should always strive to remain in Sattva Guna as Tamo Guna leads to laziness and Rajo Guna leads to fear, anxiety, and hyperactivity. He suggested the practices of reading scriptures like Ramayana, Geeta, and Mantra Meditation to remain in a relaxed and alert zone. Shri Radheshyam Das Ji again invited participants to realize the differences between reactive and proactive behavior and change our thinking by developing self-awareness. It is also necessary to change thinking to attitude and then behavior. He explained some tips for empowering the mind, which is very important for a student, by talking about Chanakya’s eight activities to be shunned completely. He spoke about ways to regulate body speech and mind to develop wisdom and satisfaction for the leaders and team members. Satisfaction, Silence, Simplicity, Self-control, and Sanctification are the five practices to calm our mind, and one must not come under the influence of senses and mind. He ended the session with various examples from Mahabharata to highlight how a calm mind acts and affects our life choices and actions very differently from a raging mind. The session was very well received, with the participation of more than 200 individuals. The participants were delighted to gain these great insights and practical knowledge in such a simplistic approach.

Talk on YOGA : The Science and Biomedical Research

Yoga is an ancient behavioral practice that allows for the development of mind-body skills including awareness / mindfulness and self-regulation of internal physiological and psychological states. This talk will review some of the basic science underlying the psychophysiology of yoga practices and describe some of the cutting edge biomedical research studies with relevance to yoga, including research on molecular biology and brain imaging. Models of psychophysiological mechanisms of action and current areas of research will be presented. We will also overview the significant research on the benefits of yoga as a therapeutic intervention, and as a preventive medicine practice for maintaining health, wellness and well-being. Research supporting the potential of yoga as a contemplative and transformative practice that enhances unitive states of consciousness and spirituality will also be reviewed.

Speaker: Dr. Sat Bir Singh Khalsa, Harvard Medical School
Time: 6:00 PM - 7:00 PM
Venue: FC Kohli Auditorium, KReSIT Building

Date : 05/03/2018
